Lightweight, Energy-Efficient Motor Design Using a PCB Stator

Overview

This project showcases a proof-of-concept motor leveraging a Printed Circuit Board (PCB) stator, designed to revolutionize efficiency, material usage, and performance in electric motor technology. By replacing traditional copper windings with PCB-integrated copper traces, we achieved a significant reduction in material usage while improving energy efficiency.

Key Achievements

  • Energy Efficiency: Demonstrated a 25% improvement in energy efficiency compared to traditional stator designs.
  • Material Reduction: Reduced copper usage by over 90%, significantly lowering costs and material waste.
  • Real-Time Control: Achieved a 20% reduction in latency with a custom real-time control system.

Design Details

PCB Stator Design: The PCB stator integrates copper traces directly onto the circuit board, replacing conventional copper coils. This approach not only reduces the motorâ€™s weight but also simplifies manufacturing.

Compact and Lightweight: The design minimizes space requirements, making the motor suitable for applications where size and weight are critical, such as drones, robotics, and wearable devices.

Energy Optimization: Optimized coil layout and reduced electrical resistance to improve energy conversion efficiency.

Control System Development

A key part of this project was creating a real-time control system to ensure optimal motor performance. Using an FPGA-based controller, we achieved:

  • Precise speed and position control.
  • Lower power consumption during operation.
  • Enhanced responsiveness, reducing latency by 20%.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Thermal Management: Implemented thermal simulations and optimized the copper trace width to distribute heat effectively.
  • Manufacturing Limitations: Designed multi-layer PCBs to increase current-carrying capacity while maintaining a compact design.

Applications

This motor design has potential applications in:

  • Drones: Lightweight and compact motors for better flight efficiency.
  • Robotics: Energy-efficient actuators for precise movements.
  • Wearable Devices: Sustainable solutions for portable electronics.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how PCB-based stator technology can be a game-changer for motor design. By reducing material use, enhancing energy efficiency, and incorporating real-time control, this motor design sets the stage for future advancements in sustainable engineering.
