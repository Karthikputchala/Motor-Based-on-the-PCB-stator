Lightweight, Energy-Efficient Motor Design Using a PCB Stator

Overview

This project demonstrates a proof-of-concept motor utilizing a Printed Circuit Board (PCB) stator to replace traditional copper windings. By integrating copper traces directly onto the PCB and optimizing thermal management, the design enhances efficiency, simplifies manufacturing, and reduces material usage and waste.

PCB Stator Design

The motor's stator is built using a 1.2 mm thick, 2-layer PCB with 0.3 mm wide copper traces. Key features include:

  • Integrated Copper Traces: Replacing traditional windings, these traces optimize energy conversion.
  • Copper-Filled Regions: These regions help dissipate heat, ensuring the motor operates without overheating.
  • Compact Structure: The thin PCB reduces the motor's weight and size, ideal for space-constrained applications like drones and robotics.
  • Simplified Manufacturing: The PCB design eliminates the need for traditional winding processes, enabling precise, repeatable production.

Control System Design

The motor's control system dynamically activates individual stator coils based on back Electromotive Force (EMF), ensuring efficient energy use and precise motor control. The control logic avoids the need for external sensors, simplifying the system and reducing hardware requirements.

Challenges and Solutions

A major challenge was managing the thermal load from the copper traces. The solution involved incorporating copper-filled regions in the PCB design to aid heat dissipation, preventing overheating during operation.

Applications

This PCB stator design is ideal for:

  • Drones: The lightweight and energy-efficient nature of the motor increases flight time and performance.
  • Robotics: Efficient, precise motors are required for optimized power consumption and extended operational time.
  • Wearable Devices: Compact motors are essential for devices like fitness trackers and smartwatches.

The design could also be applied to electric vehicles, portable electronics, and sustainable energy systems.
